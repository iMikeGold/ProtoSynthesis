System Domains

ProtoSynthesis defines multiple interconnected domains that represent the core structural layers of human systems.

Each domain functions as a distinct model of activity, but operates in relation to all other domains within a unified adaptive framework.

Human Capability Systems

Models for learning, adaptation, skill development, performance, and capability evolution.

Educational Architecture

Structures for knowledge transfer, curriculum design, progression pathways, and learning systems.

Economic Systems

Frameworks for value creation, exchange, incentives, and resource coordination.

Creative & Media Systems

Structures governing communication, storytelling, publication, and cultural expression.

Organisational Systems

Models for governance, coordination, decision-making, and operational design.

Together, these domains form the structural base of ProtoSynthesis. The architecture is defined not by the domains individually, but by the relationships between them.
