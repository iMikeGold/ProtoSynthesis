# 1. PURPOSE

The ProtoSynthesis Root Specification defines the identity, scope, boundaries, and governing principles of the ProtoSynthesis system.

It establishes:

- what ProtoSynthesis is
- what ProtoSynthesis is not
- what may exist within ProtoSynthesis
- how systems derive legitimacy within the architecture

This document defines system identity and boundary conditions.

All frameworks, standards, specifications, implementations, and derivative systems operating under ProtoSynthesis derive legitimacy from compliance with this specification.

---

# 2. SYSTEM IDENTITY

ProtoSynthesis is a layered systems architecture for structuring, modelling, governing, and evolving complex human and computational systems.

It exists to provide a coherent architectural framework through which knowledge, capability, governance, education, creativity, economics, and organisation may be represented as interconnected systems.

ProtoSynthesis is architecture.

It is not:

- a single implementation
- a product
- a software application
- a company
- a political system
- a religious system
- an ideological doctrine

It is an architectural specification system.

---

# 3. SYSTEM SCOPE

ProtoSynthesis may govern systems involving:

- knowledge representation
- learning and education
- capability development
- organisational design
- governance structures
- economic systems
- creative production systems
- media systems
- research systems
- future derivative domains

ProtoSynthesis is domain-agnostic.

It defines structural relationships, not domain content.

---

# 4. SYSTEM BOUNDARY

ProtoSynthesis governs architecture and identity.

It does not govern:

- personal beliefs
- ideological positions
- subjective interpretation
- implementation-specific decisions
- commercial operation
- legal jurisdiction
- ownership rights
- runtime execution

These concerns may exist within derived systems but are not defined by ProtoSynthesis.

ProtoSynthesis governs structure and identity, not outcome or execution.

---

# 5. GOVERNANCE MODEL

ProtoSynthesis adopts G01DN as its structural governance system.

G01DN defines:

- structural authority
- layer relationships
- inheritance rules
- constraint propagation
- validity determination

Relationship rule:

- Root Specification defines system identity and boundary conditions
- G01DN defines structural mechanics and authority behaviour

Neither overrides the other; they operate in separate authority domains.

---

# 6. SYSTEM LEGITIMACY

A framework, specification, implementation, standard, derivative system, or artefact is considered part of ProtoSynthesis only if:

- it is traceable to ProtoSynthesis architecture
- it conforms to this Root Specification
- it conforms to G01DN structural rules
- it does not violate system boundaries defined here

Any system failing these conditions is external to ProtoSynthesis.

---

# 7. STRUCTURAL PRINCIPLES

## 7.1 Coherence
All systems must maintain internal structural consistency.

## 7.2 Traceability
All artefacts must trace lineage to governing specifications.

## 7.3 Separation of Concerns
Identity, structure, execution, and representation must remain distinct.

## 7.4 Evolution
Systems may evolve without invalidating higher-order constraints.

## 7.5 Determinism
Definitions must resolve without ambiguity in interpretation.

---

# 8. DERIVATIVE SYSTEMS

ProtoSynthesis may generate derivative systems.

Derivative systems may:

- extend architectural concepts
- specialise into domains
- introduce implementation detail
- define operational models

Derivative systems may NOT:

- redefine ProtoSynthesis identity
- override Root Specification
- override G01DN authority model
- claim architectural primacy over ProtoSynthesis

---

# 9. IMPLEMENTATION RELATIONSHIP

Implementations are expressions of architecture.

They are not the architecture itself.

No deployed system, interface, or product shall override or redefine architectural truth.

Architectural truth resides in Root Specification and G01DN.

---

# 10. CHANGE GOVERNANCE

This specification may be revised only when:

- structural inconsistency is identified
- system scope requires expansion
- architectural clarity requires refinement
- long-term coherence requires correction

All changes must preserve continuity of system identity unless explicitly versioned.

---

# 11. ROOT STATEMENT

ProtoSynthesis is an architectural system for defining and governing complex interconnected systems.

Its purpose is to provide a stable framework through which complexity can be structured without dependency on any single implementation, organisation, or domain.

All valid ProtoSynthesis systems derive legitimacy from this specification.

---

# 12. STATUS

This document is the canonical Root Specification (v1.1).

It defines system identity and boundary conditions for ProtoSynthesis.

All subordinate architecture must remain compliant with this specification.
